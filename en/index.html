04 · THE SOURCING SHIFT
01 · The Sourcing Shift

Resumes show claims
Modern talent search needs evidence

Traditional tools rely on self-reported profiles, schools, employers, and keywords. They struggle to evaluate real technical capability, especially when the right evidence lives across code, papers, projects, and the open web.

FOUR PAIN POINTS
PAIN 01
Fragmented coverage
Single-platform search misses high-signal work across GitHub, Hugging Face, academic sources, projects, and public web pages.
PAIN 02
Weak proof of ability
Keywords do not prove mastery. Teams need evidence of research depth, code contribution, product execution, and domain expertise.
PAIN 03
Manual research bottlenecks
Specialized searches require repeated scoping, multi-platform research, identity checks, evidence review, and contact discovery.
PAIN 04
Siloed talent data
Every search starts from zero without shared shortlists, standardized profiles, status tracking, or a long-term talent database.

UPDATE FREQUENCY
  • GH
    GitHub Developer activity is collected continuously. High-signal accounts and repositories sync every 20–30 minutes, with full project summaries compiled every 6 hours.
  • HF
    Hugging Face Models, datasets, and Spaces update every 30 minutes. Personal and organization pages sync hourly, with incremental index refreshes every few minutes.
  • AC
    Academic platforms Paper data is updated incrementally every 30 minutes; scholar profiles are refined daily.
  • WB
    Open web and professional data Fetched in real time during active user searches, ensuring the most up-to-date live data available from public sources and third-party indexes.
